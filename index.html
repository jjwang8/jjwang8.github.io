Research

I am broadly interested in architectural mechanisms that enable genuine reasoning in neural networks and large language models, where models perform internal structured computation like search, verification, and planning rather than surface-level pattern matching.

My current work focuses on activation-free polynomial networks and transformer-style backbones with multiplicative interactions in place of standard nonlinearities, studying how design choices such as cross-receptive-field fusion, normalization, and depth shape what structures models can efficiently learn and how robustly they generalize. In parallel, I work on RLHF-style alignment with noisy preference data, exploring training schemes that explicitly evaluate and weight the reliability of learning signals instead of treating all feedback as equally trustworthy.

Going forward, I aim to design compact recurrent “reasoning cores” and world-model-based agents that can perform internal simulation, self-critique, and tool-using problem solving in open-ended environments.

Selected Projects

Activation-Free Polynomial Networks for Vision

Research project · University of Wisconsin–Madison

Designing convolutional and transformer-style backbones built from polynomial and multiplicative units instead of standard activations (e.g., ReLU, softmax), with a focus on cross-receptive-field fusion, normalization strategies, and depth scaling. I evaluate these models on ImageNet and distribution shift benchmarks to study robustness and inductive biases.

Alignment with Noisy Preference Data

Research project · RLHF / Data Quality

Investigating how label noise and annotator reliability affect reinforcement learning from human feedback, and exploring architectures and training schemes that explicitly model annotator trustworthiness instead of treating all comparisons as equally informative.

Correkt AI — Multimodal Search and LLM Agents

Co-founder & AI Lead

Built an LLM-powered search system that ingests web content, generates synthetic question–answer pairs, and uses LLM-based judges and preference models to rank results. Led the design of the core retrieval and ranking pipeline and managed a small engineering team.

Amazon — DynamoDB to Iceberg Data Pipeline

Software Development Engineer Intern

Implemented a change-data-capture pipeline from DynamoDB to Apache Iceberg to make production data efficiently queryable for downstream analytics and machine learning workloads, including schema management, batching, and failure handling.

Quantum Join Reordering for Databases

Capstone Project · Quantum Optimization

Formulated database join ordering as a QUBO problem and used quantum-inspired or variational quantum algorithms (e.g., QAOA-style approaches) to search for efficient execution plans, comparing against classical heuristics.
